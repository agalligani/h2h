hollywood2hollywood
05 Mar 2007

This weekend kicked off the planning of the journey which will take us about 6 months to complete. There is much to do in
the months ahead: sell or rent the house, accumulate cash, learn to take the BMW apart and put it back together again
in the right order. Acquire visas, inoculations, carnets and health insurance, carry out route planning, border crossing and
danger spot research, arrange vehicle shipping, dog shipping and people shipping. Yes, there is much to be done, so we
best get to it.

18 July 2007

Wow, how things have changed: out beautiful home in Hollywood is gone and we are living in a one bedroom apt up the
road. The thought that we have regressed to our early 20's has crossed my mind as we deal with all those little things that
made life easy: our own gated driveway vs a spot in the underground garage; our laundry room vs a room full of
coin-operated washer dryers; a large yard for the dogs to run in vs 4 dog walks a day to get them outside. But it all has a
purpose: the walls of the apartment are plastered with maps of our route; books are scattered the tables as we digest
places to see and areas to avoid; the closets are packed with equipment, spare parts and crap we will eventually
discard in some waste bin a Cambodia...everythings is geared toward a purpose. We ask ourselves evening after
evening: have we sacrificed too much?

25 Sep 2007

The countdown has begun. In a short 90 days we need to be in Singapore to pick up the bike after its arrival via ocean
freight. From there, a quick couple of days to recoup in the most civilized (or maybe just most westernized) city in Asia.
We continue to make progress with the checklist: bike is now ready, gear is on hand, shipping is arranged, dogs have a
welcome home and flights have been booked. But visas remain open as do vaccinations and a whole slew of loose ends.
One of the biggest challenges thus far remains passage though Iran. A quote from a prospective agent in Teheran:

"The main reason of my uncivilized delay was the "Security Issue". The ministry of foreign affairs (together with security
authorities) warned me about the security situation you would face from Pakistan border up to Kerman city. The few
guide/drivers I had spoken to were all dismayed when they learned about the exact route. They are simply concerned
about their own (and your) safety in the beginning part of the itinerary. Then I was advised by the authorities to start
corresponding directly with the Police and the Army to persuade them to provide us "Armed Escort" all the way from the
Pakistan border to Kerman city."

A military escort for only $800 per day. Excellent....
