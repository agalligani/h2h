About Us
Ever since my dad got me a purple chopper mini bike with
the pull start lawnmower engine at the no fear age of 10, I
have had an obsession with motorcycles of all kinds. My
love of travel became insatiable right after high school
and I moved overseas for the first time in 1984. Not much
of a mission then: hang out in the pubs of London’s West
End, be a punk rocker, have fun. I’m grown up now but the
stupid 18 year old never left: I just have more money to
spend and I drink vodka martinis instead of beer.

When not riding one of my bikes, I get a wicked buzz taking
our Lotus Exige or the Ducati  to the race track with my son
Alex or with Wendy, tearing up the backcountry on our
snowboards, seeing live music in the  Hollywood music
scene, or hiking with our dogs up in Griffith Park. Making this
all possible is the crazy fun, globe-trotting career I have as
a management consultant at Accenture.

My all time motorcycling highlights? Read on...

- Jumping my  mini bike over my younger siblings Evil Knevil
style. They were naïve so didn’t understand the stupidity  
involved.
- Crashing my GPz550 after about 3 months for no other
reason than I didn’t yet understand how to turn very well.
- Buying the most beautiful motorcycle ever made, the
Ducati 996, and the way my grin-induced face ache lasted
3 days after I wheelied it for the first time.
- Riding with my dad and my bro’s through Death Valley on
the maiden voyage of our now institutionalized annual
Harley ride.
- Leaving Wendy at the Greek/Turkish border during our LA
to Istanbul ride to hit an ATM on the Turkish side. The funds
in my pocket were not sufficient to pay the mandatory
duty/bribe for importing hot, leather-clad girls.
- Hitting the rev limiter in top gear at 297 km/h (184 mph or
one football field per second) on the A5 autobahn riding a
rented a Kawasaki ZX10-R. At this speed, the vacuum
created behind the race fairing feels like it is going to suck
your head off of your shoulders.
- Buying the 2006 BMW R1200GS that will take us around
the world, allowing us to fulfill an 8 year obsession.
I guess you could blame my wanderlust on my parents
and Pan Am.  If you actually want to get technical
about it though, I would have to say it was my dad and
the stack of blank airline tickets he gave me when I
was in college.  I indulged, taking my youngest sister to
St. Thomas for her 16th birthday, flying to Paris for a
weekend, disappearing in Greece with a boy for
several weeks....you name it, if Pan Am flew there and I
had enough money to eat and I could find someplace
to lay down my head, I was there.

My tastes and standards have greatly improved with
age and wisdom. The idea of sleeping in an airport,
train station or flea bag hotel with peep holes does not
hold the same charm as it once did ... in fact, I have
become more of a 5 Star Hotel with turn down service
and mint on the pillow sort of girl.  But I still love the
sense of adventure and the discovery of new things.

There is a large part of the world out there I still haven't
seen, new adventures to be had and this time a
motorcycle, instead of airplane tickets, begging to be
used.  And besides, who said I still can't experience turn
down service and a mint on my pillow...why do you
think this time I am bringing Robb????

Top Places/adventures I have had:
- going to Sydney for the first time with my dad.
- flying from LA to NY via London and getting stuck at
Heathrow airport for a day and a half without a credit
card and only $15 to my name.
- taking my sister to St. Thomas only to find out we
needed to make hotel reservations before we got
there, as opposed to after we landed, because
everything was closed
- flying to Paris for a weekend to meet a friend with only
$200 on me and still coming back with $50
-2nd time doing Paris in the springtime with Robb
- LA to Istanbul in 2000 (finishing at the Four Seasons,
with a suite upgrade, turn down service and
champagne)
